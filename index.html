Tribute to Dr APJ Abdul Kalam

A visionary leader, a great scientist, and the People's President of India.

Key Details

  • Full Name: Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam
  • Date of Birth: 15 October 1931
  • Date of Death: 27 July 2015
  • Profession: Aerospace Scientist, President of India (2002-2007)
  • Notable Works: "Wings of Fire", "Ignited Minds"

Biography

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was an eminent Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India. Born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he rose from humble beginnings to lead major scientific programs. He was known as the “Missile Man of India.”

He played a crucial role in India’s 1998 nuclear tests. Beyond his scientific contributions, Dr. Kalam was a beloved teacher and mentor who inspired millions through his books, speeches, and interactions with students. He received the Bharat Ratna for his service to the nation.

Early Life and Education

  • Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
  • Father: Jainulabdeen Marakayar (boat owner and imam).
  • Mother: Ashiamma (housewife).
  • Grew up in poverty and delivered newspapers to support his family.
  • Attended Schwartz Higher Secondary School in Ramanathapuram.
  • Graduated in Physics from St. Joseph’s College (1954).
  • Studied Aerospace Engineering at Madras Institute of Technology (1960).

Career Highlights

  • Joined DRDO in 1958; worked on India’s first satellite launch vehicle.
  • Moved to ISRO in 1963; key role in SLV-3 project.
  • Chief Scientific Adviser to the PM & Secretary, DRDO.
  • Led Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.
  • Became the 11th President of India (2002–2007).

Personal Traits

  • Humility and simplicity defined his life.
  • Firm believer in dreams and hard work.
  • Dedicated advocate for youth empowerment and education.
  • Loved teaching and mentoring students.
  • Found peace in solitude and nature.

Achievements

  • Developed India’s first satellite launch vehicle.
  • Key figure in the Pokhran-II nuclear program.
  • Author of bestsellers like "Wings of Fire".

Quotes

"Dream is not that which you see while sleeping, it is something that does not let you sleep."
"You have to dream before your dreams can come true."
